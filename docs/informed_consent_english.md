## 1. Study Title

**CittaVerse Meta-Memory Assisted Narrative Intervention for Older Adults with Mild Cognitive Impairment: A Randomized Controlled Pilot Study**

---

## 2. Background and Purpose

### 2.1 Study Background

Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) is an intermediate state between normal aging and dementia, affecting approximately 15-20% of adults aged 65 and older. About 10-15% of MCI patients progress to dementia each year, but effective interventions are currently lacking.

Reminiscence Therapy (RT) is a non-pharmacological intervention that improves cognitive function and mental health by recalling and sharing personal experiences. A 2026 meta-analysis showed that RT has moderate improvement effects on cognitive function (SMD=0.42), depressive symptoms (SMD=-0.51), and quality of life (SMD=0.45) in MCI patients.

CittaVerse is an AI-assisted narrative intervention system that enhances traditional reminiscence therapy through:
- **AI Event Extraction**: Automatic identification of key events and emotional nodes in narratives
- **Meta-Memory Guidance**: Personalized guidance questions based on cognitive science evidence (source monitoring/confidence assessment/meaning reflection)
- **Narrative Quality Scoring**: 6-dimension automatic assessment (event structure/temporal coherence/causal connection/emotional depth/sensory details/information density distribution)
- **Emotional Arousal Detection**: Dynamic adjustment of guidance strategies to match user emotional state

### 2.2 Study Purpose

**Primary Purpose**:
- To evaluate the feasibility and preliminary efficacy of CittaVerse meta-memory assisted narrative intervention on cognitive function in older adults with MCI

**Secondary Purposes**:
- To evaluate the intervention's effects on depressive symptoms, quality of life, and life satisfaction
- To evaluate the effect of meta-memory enhanced guidance (vs. standard guidance) on narrative quality
- To evaluate intervention acceptability, adherence, and safety

## 4. Participants

### 4.1 Inclusion Criteria

1. Age 60-80 years
2. MoCA score 18-25 (indicating MCI)
3. Able to use smartphone/tablet independently or with assistance
4. Able to communicate in basic language
5. Voluntary participation and signed informed consent

### 4.2 Exclusion Criteria

1. Severe dementia (MoCA <18)
2. Severe hearing or visual impairment preventing device use
3. Severe mental illness (e.g., major depression, schizophrenia)
4. Participated in another cognitive intervention study within the past 3 months
5. Other conditions deemed unsuitable by the researcher

### 4.3 Withdrawal Criteria

1. Participant voluntarily requests to withdraw
2. Serious adverse event occurs
3. Researcher determines continued participation may harm the participant
4. Very low adherence (<50% intervention completion rate)

## 5. Intervention Protocol

### 5.1 Group A (Standard Guidance)

- **Guidance Method**: Basic event recall guidance
- **Example Questions**:
  - "What happened that day?"
  - "Who were you with?"
  - "How did you feel?"

### 5.2 Group B (Meta-Memory Enhanced Guidance)

- **Guidance Method**: Meta-cognitive strategy guidance (source monitoring/confidence assessment/meaning reflection)
- **Example Questions**:
  - "How did you notice this event?" (source monitoring)
  - "How certain are you about this memory?" (confidence assessment)
  - "What does this event mean to you?" (meaning reflection)

### 5.3 Intervention Flow

| Day | Content | Duration |
|-----|---------|----------|
| D1 (03-19) | Baseline assessment + Group assignment + First intervention | 60 minutes |
| D2-D7 (03-20-03-26) | Daily narrative intervention | 15-20 minutes/day |
| D8 (03-27) | Post-test assessment + Satisfaction survey | 45 minutes |
| D21 (04-10, optional) | Follow-up assessment | 30 minutes |
