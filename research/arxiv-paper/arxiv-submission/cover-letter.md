## Summary

This technical report presents an open-source, automated narrative quality assessment tool designed for Chinese autobiographical memories in the context of reminiscence therapy (RT) for older adults with mild cognitive impairment (MCI). The key contributions are:

1. **Six-dimension scoring framework** extending the classic Autobiographical Interview (Levine et al., 2002) with temporal coherence, causal coherence, emotional depth, identity integration, and information density distribution.

2. **Chinese-language optimization** using curated vocabulary lists for temporal markers, causal connectives, self-references, and emotion words from Chinese psycholinguistic resources.

3. **Neuro-symbolic architecture** combining rule-based feature extraction with research-derived scoring heuristics, enabling deployment without cloud-based LLM dependencies.

4. **Complete open-source release** (MIT license) including source code, test suite, and example narratives at https://github.com/cittaverse/narrative-scorer.

## Relevance to cs.HC and cs.CL

The work addresses a critical gap at the intersection of HCI and NLP: how to objectively and automatically assess the quality of personal narratives produced during therapeutic interactions. The tool is deployed in an ongoing randomized controlled pilot study (N=50) and is designed for real-time feedback to both participants and clinicians.
